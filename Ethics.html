Pharmacy Law and Ethics

Introduction to Pharmacy Law and Ethics

Pharmacy law and ethics govern the safe, legal, and moral practice of pharmacy. They establish strict guidelines for drug manufacturing, prescription dispensing, and patient confidentiality, ensuring pharmacists prioritize patient well-being while avoiding illegal practices like selling substandard drugs or engaging in unethical physician commissions.

Core Legal Framework (India Context)
Pharmacy practice in India is regulated by several primary legislations:
The Pharmacy Act, 1948: Establishes the Pharmacy Council of India (PCI) and State Pharmacy Councils to regulate the profession and register qualified pharmacists.
The Drugs and Cosmetics Act, 1940 & Rules, 1945: Regulates the import, manufacture, distribution, and sale of drugs. It strictly classifies medicines into schedules (e.g., Schedule H and X for prescription and controlled drugs)
The Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances (NDPS) Act, 1985: Controls the possession, sale, and transport of habit-forming narcotics and psychotropic substances
\ The Drugs and Magic Remedies (Objectionable Advertisements) Act, 1954: Prohibits misleading advertisements and claims of miracle cures for specific diseases.
