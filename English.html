Introduction to English Typing

English typing is the process of inputting text into a computer, typewriter, or mobile device using a standard QWERTY keyboard. Mastering it through touch-typing—the method of typing using muscle memory without looking at the keys—allows for rapid communication, higher productivity, and much faster document creation

The Foundations of English Typing

To learn English typing efficiently, you must establish a solid foundation based on ergonomics, correct finger placement, and consistent practice.
Example: Google Input Tools and Barh.

1. Ergonomics and PostureProper body alignment prevents fatigue and injury during long typing sessions.
Sit straight with your feet flat on the ground. Keep your elbows bent at a 90-degree angle. Relax your wrists and let them hover slightly above the keyboard (do not rest them heavily on the desk)

2. The Home RowThe "Home Row" is the middle row of letters on your keyboard, which serves as your base position. Your fingers should always return to this row after pressing keys on the rows above or below
Left Hand: Pinky on A, Ring on S, Middle on D, Index on F. Right Hand: Index on J, Middle on K, Ring on L, Pinky on ; (semicolon). Thumbs: Rest both thumbs on the Spacebar

3. Finger Allocation
Every key on the keyboard has an assigned finger, which helps build muscle memory and increase speed. For instance, your index fingers handle the keys immediately to their left and right, while your pinkies handle peripheral keys like Shift, Enter, and Backspace. The F and J keys have small, raised bumps to help you easily locate the home row without looking down
