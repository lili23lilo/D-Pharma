Introduction to Stenography Typing

Stenography (or "steno typing") is a method of transcribing spoken words into written text at high speeds (often 100–200+ words per minute) by pressing multiple keys simultaneously. It is widely used for court reporting, legal transcription, and real-time live captioning.

How Stenography Works

Unlike a standard QWERTY keyboard where you type letter-by-letter, steno involves "chording"—striking multiple keys at the same time to form whole syllables, words, or common phrases in a single motion.

The Stenotype Machine: Features only 22 to 23 keys divided into three groups: initial consonants (left hand), vowels (thumbs), and final consonants (right hand)
Translation: A specialized steno engine translates these chord combinations into readable English (or other languages) using a dictionary.

Learning & Practising StenoMastering steno takes significant dedication and practice to build muscle memory. Depending on your goals, you can learn on a dedicated hardware keyboard or a specialized software setup.
