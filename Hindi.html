Introduction to Hindi Typing

Hindi typing is the art of writing Hindi words in the Devanagari script on a computer, mobile phone, or typewriter. It is crucial for government work, education, and digital content creation. It is primarily performed using two methods and several software.

Typing Methods

Phonetic Typing / Transliteration:This is the easiest and most popular method. In this method, the software automatically converts what you say or write in English (e.g., "Kaise ho") into Hindi ("kaise ho").
Example: Google Input Tools and Barh.

InScript Keyboard:This is the standard Hindi keyboard layout approved by the Government of India. Each key is assigned a specific Hindi letter.

Remington Layout:This is a traditional layout from the era of typewriters. Typing tests for most government jobs (such as stenographers or court clerks) use Remington (specifically the Kurti Dev font).

Popular Hindi FontsUnicode: This is the modern, global standard. Text typed in it can be read without error on any device or online.

(Examples: Mangal, Unicode).

Non-Unicode: These are old fonts often used for printing and some government exams.
(Example: Kruti Dev, Devlys)
