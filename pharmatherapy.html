Pharmacotherapy

Introduction to Pharmacotherapy

Pharmacotherapy, also known as drug therapy, is the branch of medical treatment that uses pharmaceutical drugs to prevent, diagnose, or treat diseases and alleviate symptoms. It is a cornerstone of modern healthcare, blending principles of pharmacology and biology to optimize patient healing and outcomes.

Core Concepts of Drug Therapy
Mechanism of Action: Explains exactly how a medication exerts its biochemical or physiological effects on the body.
Pharmacokinetics: The study of how medications are absorbed, distributed, metabolized, and excreted by the body.
Pharmacodynamics: How the drug interacts with the body's receptors to initiate its intended therapeutic effects

Types of Pharmacotherapy Interventions
Healthcare professionals, particularly Wikipedia Pharmacists, categorize drug therapy based on its ultimate clinical goal
Curative: Medications designed to eliminate the disease entirely (e.g., antibiotics for bacterial infections).
Preventative (Prophylaxis): Drugs used to ward off potential conditions or prevent complications (e.g., vaccines or blood pressure medication).
Supportive/Palliative: Treatment meant to relieve symptoms and improve quality of life without curing the underlying illness (e.g., analgesics for chronic pain)
